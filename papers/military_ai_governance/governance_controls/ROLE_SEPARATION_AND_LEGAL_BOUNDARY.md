# Role Separation and Legal Boundary

## Provider Obligations
Provider obligations refer to duties associated with developing, placing on the market, or making available an AI system. HHI runtime governance tooling may support provider-side documentation, logging, traceability, risk monitoring, and post-deployment evidence continuity.

## Deployer Obligations
Deployer obligations refer to duties associated with using or operating AI systems in organizational contexts. HHI runtime governance tooling may support deployer-side oversight, escalation handling, human review, operational monitoring, and audit evidence preservation.

## Operational Governance Tooling
HHI provides operational governance infrastructure. It supports telemetry, replay-capable evidence, Stop Authority records, Behavioral Drift Monitoring, Continuous Assurance, and governance observability.

## Legal Boundary
HHI does not independently determine legal compliance, certify regulatory conformity, replace legal review, replace organizational governance programs, or guarantee regulatory approval.

## Governance Assurance vs. Compliance Determination
Governance assurance means operational evidence exists to support oversight, traceability, review, and accountability.

Compliance determination means a qualified legal, regulatory, or organizational authority has evaluated whether obligations are met.

HHI strengthens governance assurance. It does not independently make compliance determinations.
