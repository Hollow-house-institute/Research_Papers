# EU AI Act Mapping Review Guardrails

These mappings are operational governance interpretations, not legal conclusions or direct statutory equivalence claims.

## Article Title Controls

- Article 9: Risk Management System
- Article 12: Record-Keeping

## Required Wording Boundary

Use:
- supports
- aligns with
- operationalizes
- provides evidence for
- strengthens traceability
- supports operational assurance

Avoid:
- requires
- mandates
- supports governance readiness
- satisfies Article
- fulfills legal requirement
- certifies compliance
- provides operational evidence

## Scope Boundaries

HHI runtime governance infrastructure provides:
- operational telemetry and evidence continuity
- traceability and replayable oversight
- runtime observability
- checksum-bound evidence integrity
- human-retained Stop Authority evidence

HHI runtime governance infrastructure does not:
- independently certify EU AI Act compliance
- replace legal review
- replace organizational governance programs
- eliminate governance risk
- guarantee regulatory approval

## Operational Interpretation Pattern

Regulatory obligation: describe the article using its canonical title and legal scope.

HHI implementation model: describe the runtime governance mechanism.

Operational enhancement: describe how the mechanism supports evidence, oversight, traceability, or assurance.

## Behavioral Drift Boundary

HHI uses Behavioral Drift as an operational governance construct describing observable deviations in runtime behavior across time. It should not be presented as a formal EU AI Act legal category.
